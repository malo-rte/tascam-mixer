= BOSS GX-700 — patch data format (per-block parameter map)
:toc: left
:icons: font

The complete byte-level layout of a GX-700 patch: the 14 sub-blocks and every
parameter offset within them. Transcribed from the *GX-700 MIDI Implementation*
(`docs/external-specs/GX-700_MI.pdf`, Tables 1–14) and **cross-validated** against a
real 100-patch bank capture (`crates/gx700/tests/fixtures/bank/`) — every block's
size and the Level/Chain assign layout were confirmed byte-for-byte against that
data.

This document is the authoritative reference so the PDFs do not need to be
re-OCR'd. It is the basis for the typed patch model and for completing
`crates/gx700/src/param.rs`.

NOTE: BOSS and Roland are trademarks of Roland Corporation. This is an
independent, unofficial project. The PDF itself is not redistributed (copyright);
only this derived parameter map is committed.

== Conventions

A patch is **14 sub-blocks**, addressed `00 00 XX 00` where `XX` is the block id
`00`..`0D`. The 4th address byte is the **parameter offset within the block**. All
data bytes are 7-bit (`0vvv vvvv`), MIDI-safe.

.Block ids and sizes (bytes), confirmed identical across all 100 captured patches
[cols="1,3,1",options="header"]
|===
| Id | Block | Size
| `00` | Level/Chain (output level, chain order, name, 4 control assigns) | 66
| `01` | Compressor   | 8
| `02` | Wah          | 13
| `03` | Distortion   | 6
| `04` | Preamp       | 10
| `05` | Loop         | 4
| `06` | Equalizer    | 7
| `07` | Speaker Simulator | 5
| `08` | Noise Suppressor  | 5
| `09` | Modulation   | 77
| `0A` | Delay        | 21
| `0B` | Chorus       | 9
| `0C` | Tremolo/Pan  | 5
| `0D` | Reverb       | 9
|===

Total ≈ 245 bytes. Each block begins with an **EFFECT ON/OFF** byte
(`0`=off, `1`=on); to bypass an effect you clear this byte (the chain order is
unchanged). A whole patch is written back as 14 separate sub-block DT1 messages.

.Encoding notations used in the DATA column of the MI tables
[cols="1,4",options="header"]
|===
| Notation | Meaning
| `0vvv vvvv` | a plain 7-bit value (`v`)
| `0000 000n` | a 1-bit flag (`n`) — typically an on/off or 2-way mode
| `0000 0nnn` / `0000 nnnn` | a small enum (`n`) — index into a value table
| `0000 dddd` (MSB) + `0000 dddd` (LSB) | an **8-bit** value split into two nibbles: `value = (MSB << 4) \| LSB`
| `0ddd dddd` (MSB) + `0ddd dddd` (LSB) | a **14-bit** value split into two 7-bit bytes: `value = (MSB << 7) \| LSB`
|===

Value ranges are written as `rawRange (displayRange)`, e.g. `00H-64H (0-100)` means
raw `0x00..0x64` displayed as `0..100`, and `00H-28H (-20dB +20dB)` means raw
`0x00..0x28` (0..40) displayed as −20..+20 dB.

== `00` — Level/Chain (66 bytes)

The patch header: master output level, the 13-byte signal-chain order, the 12-char
name, and four control assigns.

[cols="1,3,2,3",options="header"]
|===
| Offset | Field | Encoding | Value
| `00` | Output level | `0vvv vvvv` | `00H-64H` (0-100)
| `01`..`0D` | Chain 1st..13th | `0000 nnnn` | effect-type id, a permutation of `01`..`0D` (Table-1.1; `01`=Comp .. `0D`=Reverb)
| `0E`..`19` | Name char 1..12 | `0vvv vvvv` | `16H-7FH` (Table-1.4); printable ASCII `20H-7EH` in practice (e.g. `57H`='W')
| `1A`..`23` | Assign 1 | — | 10 bytes, see below
| `24`..`2D` | Assign 2 | — | 10 bytes
| `2E`..`37` | Assign 3 | — | 10 bytes
| `38`..`41` | Assign 4 | — | 10 bytes
|===

Each **control assign** is 10 bytes (offset relative to the assign's start):

[cols="1,3,2,3",options="header"]
|===
| +off | Field | Encoding | Value
| `+0`,`+1` | Target | nibble MSB+LSB → 8-bit | Target type (Table-1.2)
| `+2`,`+3` | Min | 7-bit MSB+LSB → 14-bit | follows the target parameter's min/max range
| `+4`,`+5` | Max | 7-bit MSB+LSB → 14-bit | "
| `+6` | Source | `0vvv vvvv` | `00H-43H` (Table-1.3): 0=fixed, expression/control pedals, CC#s
| `+7` | Mode | `0000 000n` | 0:NORMAL 1:TOGGLE
| `+8` | Act Lo | `0ddd dddd` | 0-127
| `+9` | Act Hi | `0ddd dddd` | 0-127
|===

NOTE: Verified against fixture `U001` (block `0`): output level `53H`=83, chain
`01..0D`, name "Whatever", then four assigns each ending `... 00 64 src 00 00 7F`
(min=0, max=100), the last assign byte at offset `41H` = byte 66.

== `09` — Modulation (77 bytes)

The largest block: one of seven modulation effects (Table-10.1) plus the
Pitch-Shifter/Harmonist voice parameters and the 36-byte harmonist scale map.
Many fields are shared between effect types (the description names every type the
field applies to).

[cols="1,3,2,3",options="header"]
|===
| Offset | Field | Encoding | Value
| `00` | Effect on/off | `0000 000n` | 0:OFF 1:ON
| `01` | Effect type | `0000 0nnn` | `00H-06H` (Table-10.1)
| `02` | Phaser stage | `0000 0nnn` | 0:4 1:6 2:8 3:10 4:12 stage
| `03` | Vibrato trigger | `0000 00nn` | 0:OFF 1:ON 2:AUTO
| `04` | Vibrato rise time | `0vvv vvvv` | `00H-64H` (0-100)
| `05` | Humanizer type | `0000 000n` | 0:AUTO 1:PEDAL
| `06` | Humanizer vowel 1 | `0000 0nnn` | 0:a 1:e 2:i 3:o 4:u
| `07` | Humanizer vowel 2 | `0000 0nnn` | 0:a 1:e 2:i 3:o 4:u
| `08` | Rate (Humanizer/Vibrato/Flanger/Phaser) | `0vvv vvvv` | `00H-64H` (0-100)
| `09` | Depth (Humanizer/Vibrato/Flanger/Phaser) | `0vvv vvvv` | `00H-64H` (0-100)
| `0A` | Flanger/Phaser manual | `0vvv vvvv` | `00H-64H` (0-100)
| `0B`,`0C` | Flanger/Phaser resonance | nibble MSB+LSB → 8-bit | (-100 +100)
| `0D` | Phaser step rate | `0vvv vvvv` | `00H-64H` (OFF, 1-100)
| `0E`,`0F` | Flanger separation | nibble MSB+LSB → 8-bit | (-100 +100)
| `10` | Flanger gate | `0vvv vvvv` | `00H-64H` (OFF, 1-100)
| `11` | Humanizer trigger | `0000 000n` | 0:AUTO 1:PEDAL
| `12` | Humanizer pedal source | `0vvv vvvv` | 0:EXP PEDAL 1:FC-200EXP 2:CC#1 .. 32:CC#31, 33:CC#64 .. 64:CC#95
| `13` | Ring modulator frequency | `0vvv vvvv` | `00H-64H` (INT, 1-100)
| `14` | Ring modulator effect level | `0vvv vvvv` | `00H-64H` (0-100)
| `15` | Ring modulator direct level | `0vvv vvvv` | `00H-64H` (0-100)
| `16` | P.S. type | `0000 00nn` | 0:SLOW 1:FAST 2:MONO
| `17`,`18`,`19` | P.S. pitch #1..#3 | `00vv vvvv` | `00H-32H` (-24 +24 semitones)
| `1A`,`1B`,`1C` | P.S. fine #1..#3 | `0vvv vvvv` | `00H-64H` (-50 +50)
| `1D` | Harmonist key | `000v vvvv` | `00H-18H` (0-24)
| `1E`,`1F`,`20` | PS & HR pre-delay #1..#3 | `0vvv vvvv` | (per-voice pre-delay) +++<!-- rows between Harmonist key @1D and Pan#2 @22; pan#1=21 -->+++
| `21`,`22`,`23` | PS & HR pan #1..#3 | `0vvv vvvv` | `00H-64H` (L100:R0 - L0:R100)
| `24`,`25`,`26` | PS & HR level #1..#3 | `0vvv vvvv` | `00H-64H` (0-100)
| `27` | PS & HR balance | `0vvv vvvv` | `00H-64H` (D100:E0 - D0:E100)
| `28` | PS & HR total level | `0vvv vvvv` | `00H-64H` (0-100)
| `29`..`4C` | Harmonist scale | `00vv vvvv` | 3 voices × 12 chromatic notes (C, Db, D, Eb, E, F, F#, G, Ab, A, Bb, B), each `00H-30H` (Table-10.2)
|===

The harmonist scale at `29`..`4C` is 36 bytes: for each note (C..B) three voice
intervals (`#1 #2 #3`), i.e. `C#1 C#2 C#3 Db#1 Db#2 Db#3 …`. Last byte `4C` =
B voice #3, which makes the block exactly 77 bytes (`00`..`4C`).

NOTE: Offsets `1E`..`20` (labelled "pre-delay") are inferred from the 3-voice
pattern — they sit between Harmonist key (`1D`, read directly) and PS & HR pan #2
(`22`, read directly), with pan #1 at `21`. Confirm against fixtures before relying
on the exact field name.

== Shared value tables

Indices referenced by the blocks above. Long enumerations (Target Type Table-1.2,
Assign Source Table-1.3, Name Table-1.4, Harmonist Scale Table-10.2) are large and
transcribed on demand from the MI PDF (pages 3, 4); their *ranges* are given inline
in the block tables above.

| 2 | Wah
| 3 | Distortion
| 4 | Preamp
| 5 | Loop

| 8 | Noise Suppressor
| 9 | Modulation

.Table-7.1 — EQ mid frequency (0..20)
100, 125, 160, 200, 250, 315, 400, 500, 630, 800 Hz; 1.00, 1.25, 1.60, 2.00, 2.50,
3.15, 4.00, 5.00, 6.30, 8.00, 10.0 kHz.

.Table-7.2 — EQ Q (0..5)
0.5, 1, 2, 4, 8, 16.

.Table-8.1 — Speaker type (0..0B)
SMALL, MIDDLE, JC-120, BUILT IN 1, BUILT IN 2, BG STACK 1, BG STACK 2,
MS STACK 1, MS STACK 2, METAL STACK, (+ remaining to `0BH`). +++<!-- last two rows transcribe on demand -->+++

.Table-10.1 — Modulation type (0..6)
0:FLANGER 1:PHASER 2:PITCH SHIFTER 3:HARMONIST 4:VIBRATO 5:RING MODULATOR
6:HUMANIZER.

.Table-10.2 — Harmonist scale interval
Raw `00H-30H` (0..48) maps to a musical interval (−24 .. +24); used by the 36 scale
bytes. Full mapping on the MI page that carries Table-10.2.

.Table-11.1 — Delay interval C (0..0A)
0:sixteenth, 1:eighth triplet, 2:dotted sixteenth, 3:eighth, 4:quarter triplet,
5:dotted eighth, 6:quarter, 7:dotted quarter, 8:half, 9:dotted half, 10:whole.

.Table-15 / Table-16 — Hi-cut / Low-cut frequency
Frequency-step tables (FLAT + a series from ~55 Hz to ~8 kHz) indexed by the
hi-cut (`00H-0FH`) and low-cut (`00H-0AH`) bytes in Chorus, Delay, and Reverb.
